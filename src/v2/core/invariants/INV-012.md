# INV-012: Keplerian Propagation Position Bound (Slice 7 — Asteroid Catalog)

## Statement

Slice 7 introduces Keplerian two-body propagation as a parallel propagation method to Hermite interpolation. Asteroid bodies (Slice 7) propagate via the Keplerian path; planetary and moon bodies (Slices 1-6) continue using Hermite. INV-012 bounds Keplerian propagation position error vs. Horizons truth:

| Body class | Cadence | Cutover bar | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Asteroid | `1 d` propagation | `100,000 km` | INV-012 new |

Notes:

- All Slice 1-6 bars (INV-008, INV-009, INV-010, INV-011) remain in force unchanged for their respective bodies.
- The asteroid bar applies only to bodies in the Slice 7 hybrid catalog (`1,000` main-belt asteroids + `8` curated famous NEAs).
- The bar was validated at `1 d` cadence vs. Horizons truth across the `2026-05-01` to `2026-07-30` window for an `18-body` representative sample.

## Continuity With Prior Invariants

INV-008 (Slice 2 bars), INV-009 (Slice 3 bars), INV-010 (Slice 4 bars), and INV-011 (Slice 6 bars) remain in force unchanged. INV-012 is additive. Heliocentric and planet-system bodies continue using their respective Hermite cadences and bars; asteroids use Keplerian propagation under INV-012.

## Truth Window

- Validation window: `2026-05-01` to `2026-07-30` (`90 days`, matching Slices 2-6)
- Truth cadence: `1 d` (`91` truth points checked per sampled body)
- Truth vectors: JPL Horizons VECTORS with `REF_PLANE='FRAME'` (equatorial / J2000 ICRF)
- Anchor epoch: `2026-05-01 00:00:00 TDB` (`JD 2461161.5`)
- Per-body anchor: Horizons VECTORS at the anchor epoch, converted to osculating elements via `cartesianToElements`
- Validation sample: `18` bodies spanning the main belt (Vesta, Pallas, Hygiea, Psyche, Ceres, Juno, Eunomia, Laetitia, Harmonia, Wanda) and all `8` curated NEAs / famous near-Earth asteroids (Bennu, Apophis, Eros, Itokawa, Ryugu, Toutatis, Geographos, Castalia)

## Check Function

```ts
assertKeplerianError(estimate: CanonicalState, truth: CanonicalState, bodyId: BodyId): void
```

- The check function applies only to Slice 7 asteroid bodies on the Keplerian path when truth is available.
- Hermite-using bodies continue routing to the existing INV-008 through INV-011 assertion path.
- In dev/test usage: throws `AssertError` with `invariantId: 'INV-012'` when position error between `estimate` and `truth` exceeds the asteroid cutover bar for `bodyId`.
- In report-mode usage: emits a structured violation entry and continues.

Violation data shape:

```ts
{
  name: 'InvariantViolation',
  invariantId: 'INV-012',
  bodyId,
  measuredErrorKm,
  barKm,
  tdbSeconds,
}
```

## Architectural Note

Keplerian propagation is the densest computational propagation in V2 to date: `1,008` bodies multiplied by continuous per-frame evaluation yields roughly `60,000` evaluations per second at `60 fps`. That budget is acceptable at Slice 7 scale on the target hardware class. A future Slice 9 catalog in the `~32,000-body` range is the scale where GPU or shader-based propagation becomes materially more attractive.

The anchor-epoch discipline is critical. Pre-research round 1 showed that propagating directly from SBDB-stored osculating elements produced order-of-magnitude error variation based on per-body epoch staleness. Bennu's SBDB epoch of `2011-01-01 TDB` yielded round-1 day-90 error of `6,739,444 km`; re-anchoring all bodies to a uniform Horizons state at `2026-05-01 TDB` reduced Bennu's day-90 error to `4,236 km`. Slice 7 therefore uses SBDB for selection and metadata, and Horizons for the propagation anchor state.

## Failure Mode

- `assertKeplerianError(estimate, truth, bodyId)` throws `AssertError` with `invariantId='INV-012'` in development for asteroid bodies
- Hermite-using bodies continue routing to the existing INV-008 through INV-011 paths
- Report-mode callers emit a structured log and continue

## Enforcement

- Slice 7 cutover test suite — validates representative asteroid states against Horizons truth at the cadence fixed by the Slice 7 fixture contract
- Supporting unit tests — verify `assertKeplerianError(...)` routing and failure semantics in isolation
- Production runtime intentionally does not call this assertion in the hot path because asteroid truth is not available there and V2's INV-004 lesson keeps expensive/assertive validation out of per-frame helpers

## Additive Invariant

INV-012 is additive. INV-008 (Slice 2 bars), INV-009 (Slice 3 bars), INV-010 (Slice 4 bars), and INV-011 (Slice 6 bars) remain in force unchanged. INV-012 adds the Slice 7 asteroid catalog without relaxing any prior invariant.

Slice 7 validates that V2 can support a second propagation method in parallel with Hermite, provided the frame remains `FRAME_HELIO_J2000_ICRF`, anchors are refreshed uniformly at the window start, and visualization-grade error bounds are enforced explicitly.
