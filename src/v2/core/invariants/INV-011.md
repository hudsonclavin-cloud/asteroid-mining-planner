# INV-011: Per-Body Interpolation Error Bound (Slice 6 — Mars System)

## Statement

Slice 6 introduces `FRAME_MARS_J2000_ICRF` and per-body cadence for the Mars system. Phobos requires sub-hourly cadence due to its `7.65 h` orbital period. Interpolation error is bounded per body at each body's own cadence:

| Body   | NAIF ID | Fixture cadence | Cutover bar |
|--------|--------:|----------------:|------------:|
| Phobos | 401     | `30 m`          | 5 km        |
| Deimos | 402     | `1 h`           | 0.5 km      |

Bars validated at `5-minute` (Phobos) and `15-minute` (Deimos) Horizons truth cadence per the Slice 6 pre-research measurement window of `2026-05-01` to `2026-07-30`.

## Continuity With Prior Invariants

INV-008 (Slice 2 bars), INV-009 (Slice 3 bars), and INV-010 (Slice 4 bars) remain in force unchanged. INV-011 is additive. Mars's existing `0.05 km` bar from INV-008 carries forward; Slice 6 does not loosen Mars's bar despite Mars now being in a planet-system rendering context.

## Truth Window

- Validation window: `2026-05-01` to `2026-07-30` (`90 days`, matching Slices 2-5)
- Phobos truth cadence: `5 m` (`17,280` truth points checked)
- Deimos truth cadence: `15 m` (`4,320` truth points checked for the `30 m` comparison and `6,480` for the `1 h` comparison)

## Measured Pre-Research Results

| Body   | Cadence | Max error | RMS error | Bar | Margin |
|--------|---------|----------:|----------:|----:|-------:|
| Phobos | `30 m`  | 0.779 km  | 0.486 km  | 5 km | 6.4x |
| Deimos | `1 h`   | 0.113 km  | 0.083 km  | 0.5 km | 4.4x |

Bars are calibrated at `4-7×` measured max with rounding for cleanliness, consistent with INV-009 and INV-010 calibration discipline.

Source measurements: `tools/slice6-research/research-report.md`.

## Runtime Check

```ts
assertInterpolationError(estimate: CanonicalState, truth: CanonicalState, bodyId: BodyId): void
```

- The runtime check reads from a unified interpolation-bar table that includes Slice 2, Slice 3, Slice 4, and Slice 6 bodies.
- The cadence per body is fixed in the constants module and is not passed at call time.
- In dev builds: throws `AssertError` with `invariantId: 'INV-011'` when position error between `estimate` and `truth` exceeds the cutover bar for `bodyId`.
- In production builds: emits a structured log entry with `invariantId: 'INV-011'` and continues.

Violation data shape:

```ts
{
  name: 'InvariantViolation',
  invariantId: 'INV-011',
  bodyId,
  measuredErrorKm,
  barKm,
  tdbSeconds,
}
```

## Architectural Note

Phobos at `30-minute` cadence is the densest fixture cadence in V2 to date (Slices 2-5 used `1 hour` as the densest). This represents a `2×` fixture-density increase per body for Phobos, which contributes to fixture-size growth (Slice 5 fixture `~2 MB` → Slice 6 fixture estimated `~3-4 MB`). The fixture-size growth curve is documented in §13 as a continuing concern; Slice 7+ SPK ingestion remains a candidate for asteroid rendering at scale.

## Failure Mode

- `assertInterpolationError(estimate, truth, bodyId)` throws `AssertError` with `invariantId='INV-011'` in development for Phobos and Deimos
- Mars uses INV-008's existing assertion path
- Production builds emit a structured log and continue

## Enforcement

- `core/interpolators` — extended for Mars-system per-body cadence and called on interpolated Phobos/Deimos states before return to any caller
- Slice 6 cutover test suite — validates Phobos and Deimos interpolated states against Horizons truth at the cadences fixed by the Slice 6 fixture contract

## Additive Invariant

INV-011 is additive. INV-008 (Slice 2 bars), INV-009 (Slice 3 bars), and INV-010 (Slice 4 bars) remain in force unchanged. INV-011 adds the two Mars-system moons without relaxing any prior invariant.

Slice 6 validates that the per-body cadence architecture from Slice 3 and Slice 4 extends to a body with sub-10-hour orbital period without forcing SPK ingestion.
