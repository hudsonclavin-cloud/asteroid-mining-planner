# INV-013: Stratified Keplerian Propagation Position Bound (Slice 8 — Catalog-Scale Asteroids)

## Summary

Stratified Keplerian propagation accuracy bounds for catalog-scale asteroid rendering.

## Statement

Slice 8 extends the Slice 7 asteroid catalog from `1,008` bodies to `10,008` bodies while retaining the same Keplerian-from-anchor propagation architecture. Pre-research showed that a single unstratified asteroid bar is too blunt at this scale: error growth varies by orbital eccentricity regime. INV-013 therefore supersedes INV-012 for asteroid bodies starting in Slice 8.

| Eccentricity band | Body class | Cadence | Cutover bar |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Band A (`e < 0.1`) | Asteroid | `1 d` propagation | `35,612.872 km` |
| Band B (`0.1 ≤ e < 0.2`) | Asteroid | `1 d` propagation | `52,970.092 km` |
| Band C (`0.2 ≤ e < 0.3`) | Asteroid | `1 d` propagation | `37,688.076 km` |
| Band D (`e ≥ 0.3`) | Asteroid | `1 d` propagation | `43,757.550 km` |

Notes:

- INV-008 through INV-011 remain in force unchanged for Hermite-using planetary and moon bodies.
- INV-012 remains the Slice 7 historical cutover artifact, but asteroid bodies entering Slice 8 are governed by INV-013.
- These bars apply to asteroid-class bodies in V2 native frames using the Slice 8 anchor-epoch discipline.

## Empirical Foundation

The bars above come directly from Slice 8 pre-research Round 3:

- Population: `10,000` main-belt asteroids by H magnitude, plus the existing `8` curated NEAs retained from Slice 7
- Sample design: `200` bodies, stratified `50/50/50/50` across bands A-D
- Anchor epoch: `2026-05-01 00:00:00 TDB` (`JD 2461161.5`)
- Truth cadence: `1 d` over the `2026-05-01` to `2026-07-30` window (`91` truth points per body)
- Anchor source: JPL Horizons VECTORS at the anchor epoch, converted to stored ecliptic-derived osculating elements

## Backward Compatibility With Slice 7

INV-013 supersedes INV-012 for asteroid bodies without invalidating Slice 7's shipped measurements. All `18` Slice 7 sampled bodies pass their Slice 8 eccentricity-band bar. The tightest case is Hygiea in Band B at `0.6667×` its INV-013 bar.

Source:

- `tools/slice8-research/data/slice7-regression-validation.json`

## Why Stratify

Slice 7's INV-012 used a single visualization-grade ceiling of `100,000 km` for the `1,008`-body catalog. Slice 8 pre-research showed that a larger population can still clear materially smaller bars when re-anchored uniformly at the window start, but the best bound depends on eccentricity regime. INV-013 therefore replaces the single asteroid bar with four eccentricity bands instead of widening Slice 7's unstratified ceiling.

## Enforcement

- Cutover-harness time only, in `tests/v2-slice8-cutover.test.mjs`
- Supporting unit tests and report-mode validation may exercise the same thresholds when truth is available
- Production runtime intentionally does not enforce INV-013 in the hot path; per V2's INV-004 lesson, expensive truth-dependent assertions stay out of per-frame helpers

## Additive Invariant

INV-013 is additive with respect to INV-001 through INV-011 and superseding with respect to INV-012's asteroid-specific role. Hermite-using bodies continue under INV-008 through INV-011. Asteroid bodies in Slice 8 use INV-013's stratified bars under the same anchor-epoch discipline established in Slice 7.
